# Managing Stress and Insomnia with Traditional Chinese Medicine

In today's fast-paced world, chronic stress and sleepless nights have become increasingly common concerns. Traditional Chinese Medicine (TCM) offers a holistic, time-tested approach to calming the mind, restoring balance, and restoring restful sleep — without the side effects of sedatives.

## The TCM View of Stress and Sleep

In Traditional Chinese Medicine, stress and insomnia are often rooted in imbalances of Qi, Blood, and the Shen (spirit). When the Liver Qi stagnates or the Heart and Kidneys lose their harmony, the mind becomes restless and sleep is disturbed.

Rather than treating insomnia as an isolated symptom, TCM looks at the whole person to identify the underlying pattern causing the disharmony.

## Common Patterns Behind Poor Sleep

### Liver Qi Stagnation
Often linked to emotional stress, frustration, and tension. Symptoms may include:
- Difficulty falling asleep
- Irritability and mood swings
- Tight shoulders and headaches

### Heart and Spleen Deficiency
Common in people who overthink or overwork. Signs include:
- Waking frequently at night
- Vivid dreams
- Fatigue and poor appetite

### Kidney Yin Deficiency
Often seen during perimenopause or after prolonged stress:
- Waking between 1–3 AM
- Night sweats and hot flashes
- Restlessness and anxiety

## How Acupuncture Helps

Acupuncture regulates the nervous system by activating parasympathetic pathways — the body's "rest and digest" mode. Regular sessions can:
- Calm the mind and ease anxiety
- Regulate cortisol and melatonin rhythms
- Relieve muscle tension from chronic stress
- Improve both sleep quality and sleep depth

Commonly used points include Shen Men, An Mian, and Yin Tang, each known for their calming and sleep-supporting effects.

## Lifestyle Practices for Better Sleep

TCM emphasizes daily habits as a foundation for long-term wellness. Simple practices that support sleep include:
1. Keeping a consistent sleep schedule, ideally before 11 PM
2. Limiting screens and stimulants in the evening
3. Practicing gentle Qi Gong or stretching before bed
4. Eating warm, easily digested meals at dinner
5. Taking brief daily walks in natural light
